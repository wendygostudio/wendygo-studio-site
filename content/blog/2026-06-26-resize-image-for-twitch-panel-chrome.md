Twitch panels sit below your channel bio—small rectangular boxes where streamers drop thumbnails, call-to-actions, and quick links. They're 320×160 px, which is one of the most awkward aspect ratios to work with. Most image editors assume square or wide formats. A source photo that looks fine at 16:9 gets mangled when you try to squeeze it into a 2:1 ultra-wide rectangle.

This guide shows how to resize images for Twitch panels using **FrameForge**, a Chrome extension that handles the math for you and keeps your images local (no uploading to a server).

## Twitch Panel Dimensions

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Panel size** | 320×160 px |
| **Aspect ratio** | 2:1 (ultra-wide) |
| **Max file size** | 10 MB |
| **Formats** | JPG, PNG, GIF, WebP |
| **Number of panels** | Up to 3 rows (unlimited, but only 3 visible per row) |

Panels are often overlooked in channel design, but they're the first thing a visitor sees after your header image. A poorly cropped panel looks unfinished.

## The Cropping Challenge

A 2:1 rectangle cuts off a lot vertically. If you have a portrait photo (9:16) or even a 3:4 headshot, the panel frame will show only a thin horizontal slice. Landscapes (16:9) are closer but still require a specific crop to avoid leaving dead space.

**FrameForge includes a Twitch panel preset**, so you don't have to manually calculate the dimensions or experiment with crop ratios. Load your image, select the preset, and adjust the crop overlay.

## Step-by-Step: Resize for Twitch Panels with FrameForge

1. **Install FrameForge** from the Chrome Web Store. Pin the icon to your toolbar.

2. **Open your image.** Click the FrameForge icon, then click **Open image** or drag your file onto the canvas.

3. **Select Twitch Panel from the Platform dropdown.** The canvas locks to 320×160 px immediately.

4. **Adjust the crop.** Drag the overlay to frame the part of your image that matters most—your face, your logo, or the key visual element. FrameForge lets you see the crop live.

5. **Export.** Click **Export**. FrameForge saves the resized image to your Downloads folder, ready to upload to your Twitch channel settings.

## Why Panels Matter on Twitch

Panels are customizable boxes where you can link to:
- Your Discord server
- Ko-fi or Patreon
- Stream schedule or social media
- A portfolio or Linktree

Each panel is small, but collectively they fill the space below your channel header. Visitors see them before they see much else. A crisp, well-cropped panel image looks professional. A stretched or off-center crop looks like an afterthought.

Because panels are so narrow, you'll often need to crop tighter than you'd expect. A photo that looks balanced at 16:9 might need the edges trimmed significantly to work at 2:1.

## Common Twitch Panel Uses

- **Channel logo or icon** — 320×160 works well for a slightly letterboxed version of your logo
- **Social links** — Text + icon (Discord, Twitter, Instagram)
- **Sponsorship or product** — Game cover art, software interface, merchandise photo
- **Schedule or countdown** — Text overlay on a solid background (though FrameForge doesn't add text; you'd do that in another tool)
