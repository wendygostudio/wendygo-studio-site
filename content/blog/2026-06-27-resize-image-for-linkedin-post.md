# How to Resize an Image for LinkedIn Posts in Chrome (1200×627)

LinkedIn renders feed images at a narrow horizontal crop, similar to X and Facebook. Square photos lose the vertical edges. Portrait shots get squeezed. The fix: post at 1200×627 px (roughly 1.9:1) and the image displays in full in the LinkedIn feed preview without unexpected cropping.

## LinkedIn Image Size Requirements

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Recommended size | 1200×627 px |
| Aspect ratio | ~1.9:1 (approximately 16:8.4) |
| Max file size | 10 MB (JPG/PNG) |
| Accepted formats | JPG, PNG, WebP |

## Why LinkedIn Crops Images

LinkedIn applies a center-crop when rendering inline images in your feed. The platform shows roughly a 1.9:1 slice from the center of whatever you attach.

A square (1:1) photo at 1200×1200 gets cropped to around 1200×627, cutting the top and bottom thirds. A portrait shot (9:16) gets center-cropped even more aggressively, showing only a thin vertical slice in the preview.

Posting at 1200×627 eliminates the mismatch. Your image is already at the right proportions for LinkedIn's feed, so the preview shows the full composition without any unexpected trim.

## Which Fill Mode for LinkedIn?

**Crop to fill** (Recommended) — The entire 1200×627 canvas is filled; excess from the sides (for portrait sources) or top/bottom (for landscape sources) is trimmed. Decide exactly what stays in frame by dragging the overlay.

**Fit with padding** — The full source image is visible, with bars filling the 1200×627 frame. This often looks unintentional in a professional feed.

**Stretch to fill** — The image distorts horizontally. Avoid unless distortion is your deliberate choice.
