Most Pomodoro timers fail you the same way: you set 25 minutes, the alarm rings, you close the tab, and by the next morning you've forgotten the extension exists. SlimeForge fixes this with a virtual slime pet that lives inside your browser and grows only when you focus.

## What Is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique splits your work into intervals — typically 25 minutes of focused work followed by a short break. After four cycles, you take a longer break. It's one of the most researched time-management methods because it makes the act of sitting down to work feel manageable and bounded.

The problem isn't the technique. It's keeping a timer you actually open every day.

## How SlimeForge Works as a Pomodoro Timer

SlimeForge runs entirely in your Chrome browser. No account, no server, nothing sent anywhere.

When you open the extension, you pick your session length:

- **15 minutes** — sprint mode for small tasks
- **25 minutes** — classic Pomodoro
- **45 minutes** — deep work blocks
- **60 minutes** — flow state sessions

Short and long breaks are included. SlimeForge tracks your streaks and session stats so you can see how consistent you've been over the week.

## The Virtual Pet That Makes You Come Back

Here's the difference from a plain timer: your slime hatches after your first completed session.

Every focus session you complete earns you Brasas 🔥 — the in-extension currency. Your slime grows as you accumulate sessions. Ignore the extension for a few days and your pet doesn't grow. Open it and complete a session and it does. That feedback loop is surprisingly effective at turning "I should focus" into "I want to open SlimeForge."

There are 16 slime species to discover. You don't buy species — you find them by focusing.

SlimeForge adds several layers on top of the basic timer:
