Best Pomofocus Alternatives in 2026 — Free & on Chrome

Pomofocus is a clean, browser-based Pomodoro timer — but it is a website, not a Chrome extension. It does not follow you between tabs, does not track streaks locally, and offers no reward loop for finishing sessions. These five alternatives fix that.

July 19, 2026

What to Look for in a Pomofocus Alternative

Before picking a replacement, decide what is actually missing. Pomofocus does one thing well: a simple, clean countdown you open in a tab. The most common reasons people switch are:

  • Chrome extension access — toolbar popup without switching tabs
  • Offline capability — works without an internet connection
  • Stats and streaks — persistent focus history over days and weeks
  • Customizable timer lengths — beyond a fixed 25-minute default
  • Motivation beyond the countdown — something that makes finishing sessions feel worth it

The alternatives below are ranked by how well they solve the problem of not persisting across tabs — the most common reason people leave Pomofocus.

The 5 Best Pomofocus Alternatives in 2026

1
SlimeForge
Our pick

Best for: Chrome extension with gamification and offline support

SlimeForge is a Pomodoro timer Chrome extension that lives in your toolbar and opens on any tab without switching pages. It supports four session lengths — 15, 25, 45, and 60 minutes — so you can match the timer to your actual energy level rather than a fixed formula.

Every completed session grows a virtual slime pet. The pet hatches from an egg on your first session, evolves through 16 species, and earns Brasas 🔥 — the in-extension currency for unlocking missions, mini-games, and new slime variants. The pet can also wander your browser tabs and chat in six languages using on-device AI.

Runs entirely offline. No account required. All progress, streaks, and your pet are stored locally. Free tier includes the full timer, virtual pet, and session stats. A 5-day Pro trial is available.

2
Forest

Best for: Mobile-first focus with a visual commitment mechanic

Forest is a well-known focus app on iOS and Android. You plant a virtual tree when a session starts; it dies if you leave the app before the timer ends. Trees accumulate over time, and Forest donates to real tree-planting programs using coins earned in the app.

There is no official Forest Chrome extension. It is the strongest option if your phone — not your browser — is your primary source of distraction. If you are looking for Forest app alternatives that work in Chrome, browser-native extensions are a better starting point.

3
Focus To-Do

Best for: Task management integrated with Pomodoro session tracking

Focus To-Do combines a Pomodoro timer with a full task list. You assign sessions to specific tasks and it records how many sessions each task consumed. Available on Windows, Mac, iOS, Android, and as a Chrome extension. A practical choice if you want to connect focus time directly to project or deadline tracking.

4
Be Focused

Best for: Mac and iOS users who want Apple ecosystem integration

Be Focused is a Pomodoro app for Mac and iOS with customizable session lengths, break durations, and Apple Reminders integration. No Windows or Android version. A clean, minimal option if you work exclusively in the Apple ecosystem.

5
Toggl Track

Best for: Freelancers already using Toggl for billable time tracking

Toggl Track is primarily a time tracker for freelancers and agencies, but includes a Pomodoro mode. If you already use Toggl to log project hours, enabling Pomodoro mode adds session-based focus on top of your existing time records. Not a purpose-built focus tool, but useful if time tracking is your primary workflow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a Pomofocus Chrome extension?

Pomofocus itself does not offer an official Chrome extension. It works in any browser tab but does not persist across tabs or appear in the toolbar. For toolbar-based Pomodoro access, a dedicated Chrome extension like SlimeForge or Focus To-Do is the closest alternative.

What is the best free Pomodoro timer Chrome extension in 2026?

SlimeForge and Focus To-Do are the strongest free options. SlimeForge includes a full Pomodoro timer, virtual pet, session streaks, and stats at no cost. Focus To-Do offers free plans with task management integration.

What does Pomofocus lack compared to Chrome extensions?

Pomofocus is a tab-based web app — it does not run in the toolbar or follow you between pages. It has no offline mode, streak system, or gamification. A dedicated Chrome extension solves all three gaps.

Is SlimeForge completely free?

The core Pomodoro timer, virtual pet, session stats, and streaks are free with no time limit. A 5-day Pro trial unlocks additional features, and free features remain available indefinitely after the trial ends.

Can I use a Pomodoro timer while browsing without switching tabs?

Yes — Chrome extensions like SlimeForge open from the toolbar as a popup, so you can start or check your timer without leaving your current tab. Pomofocus requires switching to its own tab to interact with it.
