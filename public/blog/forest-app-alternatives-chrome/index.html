Best Forest App Alternatives for Chrome in 2026

Forest has made virtual tree-planting synonymous with focus timers — but it's not the only option in Chrome. This guide compares four alternatives honestly: what each one does well, where it falls short, and who should pick it.

July 16, 2026

For years, Forest has been the default recommendation for people who want a visual reward tied to staying focused. Plant a virtual tree, stay off distracting apps, watch it grow. The concept works. But the Chrome extension is a companion to the mobile app — the fuller experience lives on your phone, and cross-device sync requires the paid plan.

If you spend most of your focused work time in a browser and want something that runs natively there, or you simply want a different approach to keeping yourself on task, the alternatives below are all worth considering.

What to Look For in a Forest Alternative

Before comparing options, it helps to be clear about what you actually need from a focus timer. The main dimensions:

  • Motivation style: Do you respond better to positive reward (something growing, unlocking) or restriction (blocking sites you'd visit otherwise)?
  • Complexity: Do you want a single countdown, or a system with sessions, breaks, streaks, and progression?
  • Privacy: Does the tool require an account, send data to a server, or sync to a cloud service?
  • Cost: What feature tier is free, and what requires a subscription?

Alternative 1: Marinara Timer

Marinara Timer
Minimalist

Marinara Timer is a browser-tab Pomodoro timer — no extension needed. Open the URL, set your intervals, and a large countdown fills the screen. It's open source, free, and shareable (you can send a link to a running timer for team Pomodoros).

Strengths
  • Free and open source
  • No account, no install
  • Shareable timer links for team use
  • Completely predictable — it's just a clock
Weaknesses
  • No gamification or visual reward
  • No session tracking or streaks
  • Nothing to make you open it again tomorrow
Best for: People who find gamification distracting and want only a countdown. Also useful for paired focus sessions with a teammate.

Alternative 2: Strict Workflow

Strict Workflow
Restrictive

Strict Workflow combines a Pomodoro timer with automatic site blocking. During work sessions, a configurable blocklist (Reddit, X, YouTube, etc.) becomes inaccessible. When the break starts, blocked sites are restored. It doesn't ask you to stay focused — it enforces it.

Strengths
  • Hard blocks remove temptation entirely
  • Free Chrome extension
  • Configurable blocklist
Weaknesses
  • Blunt — no flexibility during sessions
  • Problem if your work legitimately needs social sites
  • No progression or reward system
Best for: People who know they'll break focus if given the chance and want the extension to enforce the rule, not just suggest it.

Alternative 3: Momentum

Momentum
New-tab replacement

Momentum replaces every new tab with a calm landscape, the time, and a daily focus prompt ("What is your main focus today?"). It's less about session-level timing and more about daily intention-setting. A paid plan adds to-do lists, integrations, and additional widgets.

Strengths
  • Beautiful, low-distraction new-tab experience
  • Daily focus goal stays visible every time you open a tab
  • Passive — doesn't require you to do anything
Weaknesses
  • Not a Pomodoro timer — no session tracking
  • Most useful features are paid
  • Different philosophy than timed intervals
Best for: People who want ambient focus cues rather than timed sessions. Works well alongside a separate timer, not instead of one.

Alternative 4: SlimeForge — Gamified Pomodoro With a Virtual Pet

SlimeForge
Editor's pick

SlimeForge is a Pomodoro timer Chrome extension built around a virtual slime pet. After your first completed session, a slime hatches inside the extension. Each session earns Brasas 🔥 that help it grow. There are 16 species to discover, missions, crafting, and mini-games for breaks — all running locally in your browser, nothing uploaded to any server.

Strengths
  • Free to start, 5-day PRO trial
  • Timer (15/25/45/60 min) + pet + streaks + missions
  • Ethical gacha: real money never buys random outcomes
  • Gemini Nano on-device chat in 6 languages
  • No account, no cloud sync, fully local
Weaknesses
  • More to explore than a minimal timer
  • Doesn't block distracting sites
  • Virtual pet, not a virtual tree — different aesthetic
Best for: People who've tried plain timers and stopped using them. Students, developers, and writers who do long screen-based work and want a focus habit that sticks.

The common thread between Forest and SlimeForge is using something that grows as a focus incentive. SlimeForge extends that idea with more depth — 16 species, crafting, missions, and Gemini Nano on-device chat — while running entirely locally. No account required, no data sent anywhere.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Forest available as a Chrome extension?

Yes, Forest has a Chrome extension, but its full feature set — including cross-device sync and the real tree planting program — requires the paid subscription. The mobile app is the primary product; the extension is a companion.

What is the best free alternative to Forest for Chrome?

It depends on what you value. Marinara Timer is the best free option if you want a pure, no-frills Pomodoro countdown. SlimeForge is the best free option if you want a gamified timer with a virtual pet that grows with every session you complete.

Does SlimeForge block distracting websites like Forest?

No — SlimeForge doesn't block sites. It motivates through positive reward (Brasas, your growing slime, streaks and missions) rather than restriction. If site blocking is your priority, Strict Workflow is the better fit.

Does SlimeForge require an account or subscription?

No account is required. SlimeForge runs entirely locally in your browser — no data is uploaded to any server. A 5-day PRO trial is available to explore the full feature set before deciding.

Can I use SlimeForge offline?

Yes. The timer, virtual pet, streaks, and Gemini Nano on-device chat all work without an internet connection after installation.
